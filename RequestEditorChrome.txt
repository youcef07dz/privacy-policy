### Privacy Policy for Request Editor

**Effective Date:** May 27, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how **Request Editor**  handles information when you use our Chrome extension.

#### 1. Data Collection and Use

We are committed to your privacy. **Request Editor** is designed to function entirely locally on your device.

* **No Data Transmission:** We do not collect, store, transmit, or share any of the network requests, headers, or body content that you intercept, inspect, or modify using this extension.
* **Local Processing:** All debugging operations—including the interception of HTTP requests and the application of modifications—occur locally within your browser session.
* **Storage:** Any user-defined configurations, such as custom headers or UI preferences, are stored locally on your machine using the Chrome `storage` API. This information is never sent to our servers.



#### 2. Permissions

To provide its functionality, this extension requires the following permissions:

* **`debugger`**: Used exclusively to attach to your browser tab to intercept and modify HTTP network traffic as part of the debugging process.
* **`activeTab`**: Used to ensure the extension only interacts with the webpage you are currently viewing and have explicitly triggered.
* **`storage`**: Used to persist your local user settings and configurations.



#### 3. Data Security

Because no data is transmitted to or stored on external servers, the risk of unauthorized data access is minimized. Your intercepted data remains on your local machine and is cleared when you close the extension or the browser tab.

#### 4. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected in this document, and the "Effective Date" will be updated accordingly.
